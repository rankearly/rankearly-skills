# Phase 1: Winnability Rubric

## Verdict criteria

| Verdict | Criteria | Action |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Winnable** | 3+ positive signals and 0-1 barriers | Continue to Phase 2 |
| **Challenging** | 2 positive signals and 2 barriers | Continue with strategy notes |
| **Hard-to-Win** | 0-1 positive signals and 3+ barriers | Recommend skip, but user can override |

## Positive signals (opportunity)

Inspect the saved top-10 SERP composition to identify these:

- **Small sites or UGC in top 10**: Forums, Reddit threads, Quora answers, niche blogs, or low-DR sites holding positions. Indicates the SERP is not locked down by authority sites.
- **Stale content**: Pages that appear outdated based on snippet dates, title dates, or clearly old information. Last updated 6+ months ago when detectable.
- **Mixed or fractured intent**: The top 10 serves different intents (some informational, some commercial, some navigational). This creates a wedge opportunity to serve one intent better than the mixed results.
- **Content format gap**: Searchers appear to want one format (e.g., a comparison table, a tool, a video) but the top results provide something else (e.g., generic blog posts).
- **Thin or generic content**: Top results have shallow, boilerplate, or obviously AI-generated content that a well-researched piece could easily surpass.

## Barriers (negative signals)

- **Authority-dominated SERP**: High-authority domains (official documentation, .gov sites, Wikipedia, major platforms like HubSpot, Forbes, Healthline) hold 3+ of the top 10 positions.
- **High keyword difficulty**: The keyword difficulty score from the keyword overview data is high (typically 70+), indicating strong backlink profiles across ranking pages.

## How to assess

1. Walk through each of the top 10 results from the saved SERP snapshot
2. For each result, check the URL domain, title, and snippet against the signal definitions above
3. Count positive signals and barriers separately
4. Apply the verdict rubric
5. Write the search intent brief based on what the top results collectively suggest Google rewards for this query

The assessment should be based on observable evidence from the SERP data, not speculation. If a signal is ambiguous, note it but don't count it.
