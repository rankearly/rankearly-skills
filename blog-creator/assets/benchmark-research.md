# Benchmark / Original Research Report

> When to use: Data studies, industry benchmarks, survey results, original research
> Minimum length: 2,000 words

## Bones (required)

1. **Key findings summary** — the headline numbers up front, before the methodology. Readers want the insights first.
2. **Methodology** — transparent about data source, sample size, time period, analysis method, and limitations. This is what separates research from opinion.
3. **Findings** — each finding gets its own section with data, visualization, and interpretation. The number of findings should match the data — don't force 4 if you have 6 interesting ones.

## Moves (pick 1-3 as relevant)

- **What this means for [audience]** — practical implications, segmented by persona if useful
- **How to use these benchmarks** — actionable steps for applying the data
- **Comparison to prior periods** — when trend data is available
- **Outliers and surprises** — counterintuitive results deserve extra attention
- **FAQ** — questions about methodology or interpretation
- **TL;DR** — 3-4 bullet summary of the most important numbers

## Guidance

- Every finding should answer "so what?" — don't just present data, interpret it for the reader.
- Visualizations should earn their place. A chart that shows a clear pattern is worth a thousand words. A chart that restates a single number is noise.
- Be honest about limitations. Acknowledging what the data can't tell you builds more trust than pretending it's comprehensive.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't round numbers vaguely ("about 50%"). Precision signals rigor.
- Don't bury the methodology so deep that readers question the data's credibility. But don't lead with it either — findings first.
- Don't present every data point as equally important. Lead with the finding that would make someone stop scrolling.
