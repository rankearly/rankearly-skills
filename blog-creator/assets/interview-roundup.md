# Interview / Expert Roundup

> When to use: Expert interviews, multi-expert roundups, thought leader Q&As
> Minimum length: 1,500 words

## Bones (required)

1. **Expert perspectives** — each expert's view presented with their credentials and substantive insight. Can be organized per-expert OR per-theme — choose whichever structure makes the collective wisdom clearer.
2. **Synthesis** — what the experts agree on, where they diverge, and what the reader should take away. This is what elevates a roundup above a list of quotes.

## Moves (pick 1-3 as relevant)

- **The key question** — frame the central question or debate that prompted the roundup
- **Expert table** — quick-reference of who's featured, their role, and what they're known for
- **Pull quotes** — the most quotable insight from each expert, highlighted visually
- **Points of disagreement** — where experts diverge is often more interesting than where they agree
- **Actionable takeaways** — synthesized advice the reader can act on
- **TL;DR** — consensus + notable dissent in a few sentences
- **FAQ** — meta-questions (how experts were selected, methodology)

## Guidance

- The synthesis section is what makes this format valuable. Without it, you just have a list of quotes. With it, you've created new understanding.
- If organizing per-expert, vary the section lengths based on how much each expert contributed. Don't pad a brief insight to match a detailed one.
- If organizing per-theme, credit each perspective clearly so the reader knows who said what.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't give every expert an identical section structure (headshot, background, quote, 300 words). Match the format to what each expert actually contributed.
- Don't hide disagreements to present a false consensus. Divergent views are the most interesting part.
- Don't include experts who don't add a distinct perspective just to increase the headcount.
