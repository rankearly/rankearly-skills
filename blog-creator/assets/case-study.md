# Case Study

> When to use: Success stories, client results, before/after transformations
> Minimum length: 1,200 words

## Bones (required)

1. **The challenge** — what problem was faced, why it mattered, what the stakes were. Set up the story.
2. **What was done** — the strategy and execution. Be specific enough that someone could learn from it. The structure should follow the actual story — 1 phase or 5 phases, whatever happened.
3. **The results** — quantified outcomes with real numbers. Before vs. after. This is the payoff.

## Moves (pick 1-3 as relevant)

- **Key results summary** — a quick metrics box near the top for readers who want the headline numbers immediately
- **Key takeaways** — actionable lessons others can apply to their own situation
- **Context / background** — company size, industry, timeline — when the reader needs it to judge applicability
- **What didn't work** — failed approaches tried before the winning strategy. Adds credibility.
- **Quote from the subject** — a pull quote adds authenticity, but only if it says something substantive
- **FAQ** — when the case study raises questions about methodology or applicability

## Guidance

- This is a story. Let it read like one. The challenge should create tension, the execution should build momentum, the results should deliver satisfaction.
- Specificity is everything. "Increased traffic" is weak. "Grew organic traffic from 2,400 to 18,000 monthly sessions in 6 months" is a case study.
- The execution section is where most case studies go wrong — they stay vague to protect proprietary methods. Push for as much tactical detail as the subject allows.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't force a 3-phase structure. Some transformations happen in one bold move. Others unfold over many iterations. Follow the real story.
- Don't open every section with a statistic. The challenge section is about stakes and tension — lead with the human problem, not a number.
- Don't pad with generic business context. Only include background that helps the reader judge whether this case applies to them.
