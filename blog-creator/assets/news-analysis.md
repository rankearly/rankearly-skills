# News Analysis

> When to use: Industry news commentary, trend analysis, event breakdowns
> Minimum length: 1,000 words

## Bones (required)

1. **What happened** — factual summary with source citations. Separate facts from interpretation.
2. **Why it matters** — context, who's affected, how it fits into broader trends. This is your analysis, clearly labeled as such.
3. **What to do about it** — actionable advice for the reader based on the news.

## Moves (pick 1-2 as relevant)

- **What to expect next** — short-term and longer-term implications, specific predictions
- **Counter-arguments** — alternative interpretations or reasons the news might be less significant than it appears
- **Timeline** — sequence of events leading to this moment, when context helps
- **FAQ** — common reader questions about the news
- **TL;DR** — the news + your take in 2-3 sentences

## Guidance

- Speed matters for this format. Get to the point fast — the reader already knows something happened and wants your angle.
- Clearly separate facts (sourced) from analysis (your interpretation). Don't blend them.
- The "what to do" section is what makes this more than a news recap. Be concrete: "audit your X," "switch to Y," "watch for Z."

## Anti-patterns

- Don't write a 500-word introduction before telling the reader what happened. Lead with the news.
- Don't speculate without labeling it as speculation. "We expect..." is honest. Stating predictions as facts is not.
- Don't force long-term implications when the news is minor. Some events only have short-term relevance — that's fine.
