# How-To Guide

> When to use: "How to..." queries, step-by-step tutorials, process guides
> Minimum length: 1,500 words

## Bones (required)

1. **Sequential steps** — Numbered steps in logical order. Each step has a clear action and expected outcome. The number of steps should match the actual process — don't force 3 steps or 7 steps.
2. **Prerequisites** — What the reader needs before starting (tools, accounts, knowledge). Appears before the steps.

## Moves (pick 2-3 as relevant)

- **Context / why this matters** — brief background for readers who need motivation before diving in
- **Common mistakes** — pitfalls to avoid, ideally placed near the step where they occur (or as a standalone section if they span multiple steps)
- **Verification** — how to confirm the steps worked, what success looks like
- **Troubleshooting** — what to do when things go wrong at specific steps
- **FAQ** — questions that don't fit naturally into the step flow
- **TL;DR / summary box** — quick-reference version of the steps for returning readers
- **Variations** — alternative approaches for different situations (e.g., "If you're using X instead of Y...")

## Guidance

- Step titles should be action-oriented: "Configure the DNS records" not "DNS configuration."
- Some steps are simple (one sentence). Others are complex (multiple paragraphs with screenshots). Match depth to the actual difficulty.
- If a step has a gotcha, put the warning right there — not in a separate "Common Mistakes" section the reader might never reach.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't add a "What Is [Topic]?" section when the reader clearly searched "how to do X" — they know what it is, they want instructions.
- Don't stretch a 5-step process into 10 steps by splitting simple actions. "Click Save" is not a step.
- Don't force a FAQ if the steps and troubleshooting already cover everything.
