# Glossary / Definition

> When to use: "What is [X]?" queries, term definitions, concept explainers
> Minimum length: 600 words

## Bones (required)

1. **Clear definition up front** — the first paragraph answers "What is [X]?" in plain language. This is the featured snippet target. No jargon, no preamble.
2. **How it works or how it's used** — move from definition to practical understanding. Make the concept concrete.

## Moves (pick 1-3 as relevant)

- **Simple terms / analogy** — a real-world comparison for readers who need an intuitive grasp
- **Examples** — concrete instances showing the concept in action. 2-3 is typical, but use as many as the concept needs.
- **Why it matters** — impact on the reader's work or industry, with data if it exists
- **[Term] vs [Related Term]** — a comparison table when the term is commonly confused with another
- **Common misconceptions** — myths vs. reality, especially useful for misunderstood concepts
- **FAQ** — "Is X the same as Y?" and other natural follow-up questions

## Guidance

- The definition paragraph is the single most important element. Spend extra effort making it precise, accessible, and complete in 2-3 sentences.
- Some concepts need 600 words. Others need 1,500. Don't stretch a simple definition to fill space, and don't compress a complex one to stay short.
- If someone reads only the first paragraph and leaves, they should still have gotten value.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't bury the definition below a "Why [Term] is Important" introduction. Answer the question first.
- Don't pad a 600-word concept to 1,500 words with filler sections. Short and clear wins.
- Don't explain the term using other jargon the reader probably doesn't know either.
