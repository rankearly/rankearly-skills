# Template / Checklist

> When to use: Downloadable templates, step-by-step checklists, worksheets, swipe files
> Minimum length: 1,000 words (plus the template/checklist itself)

## Bones (required)

1. **The template or checklist itself** — the actual deliverable the reader came for. Copy-paste ready, organized into logical sections. This is the core — everything else supports it.
2. **Brief usage instructions** — how to use the template effectively. 3-5 steps max.

## Moves (pick 1-3 as relevant)

- **Who this is for** — personas and situations where this template applies
- **Why each item matters** — explanations for the non-obvious checklist items (skip obvious ones)
- **Filled-in example** — a completed version showing what good looks like
- **Common mistakes** — what people get wrong when using this type of template
- **FAQ** — questions about customization, frequency of use, etc.
- **Variations** — how to adapt the template for different contexts or team sizes

## Guidance

- The template should appear early, not after 1,000 words of context. The reader came for the thing — give it to them, then explain.
- Not every checklist item needs a paragraph of justification. Explain the non-obvious ones. Skip the self-explanatory ones.
- A filled-in example is often more useful than a page of instructions.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't write a full article and attach a checklist at the end. The checklist IS the article.
- Don't add a "Why Checklists Matter" section. The reader already decided they want one.
- Don't explain every item at equal length. Some items are self-evident ("Set a deadline"). Others need context ("Validate against schema version 3.2").
