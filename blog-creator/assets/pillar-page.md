# Pillar Page / Ultimate Guide

> When to use: Comprehensive topic coverage, hub pages linking to cluster content
> Minimum length: 2,500 words

## Bones (required)

1. **Definition and context** — What the topic is, why it matters now. Accessible to beginners but not patronizing.
2. **Core concepts** — 3+ deep sections, each covering a major facet of the topic. Every section should be strong enough to stand alone as a mini-article.
3. **Internal linking** — Each core concept section links to a cluster article for deeper exploration. This is a hub page.

## Moves (pick 2-4 as relevant)

- **Table of contents** — useful when the article exceeds 3,000 words or has 5+ major sections
- **Advanced strategies** — expert-level techniques for readers who already know the basics
- **Tools & resources** — curated list organized by category, with brief descriptions
- **FAQ** — address common search queries not covered in the main sections
- **Timeline / history** — when the topic has evolved significantly over time
- **Comparison table** — when multiple approaches, tools, or methods need side-by-side evaluation
- **TL;DR / summary box** — a 2-3 sentence distillation at the top for scanners

## Guidance

- Each core concept section should lead with the key insight, not background. Get to the point, then support it.
- This format earns its length through depth, not padding. If a subtopic only needs 200 words, give it 200 words.
- Name sections after the actual concepts, not generic labels like "Core Concept 1."

## Anti-patterns

- Don't title it "The Ultimate Guide to X." Google has seen that title millions of times. Find a more specific angle.
- Don't force every section into the same structural rhythm. Some sections are best as narrative, others as lists, others as tables. Let the content dictate the format.
- Don't add a FAQ section just to have one. If the main sections already answer the common questions, skip it.
- Don't pad thin subtopics to match the length of substantial ones. Uneven section lengths are fine — they signal honest coverage.
